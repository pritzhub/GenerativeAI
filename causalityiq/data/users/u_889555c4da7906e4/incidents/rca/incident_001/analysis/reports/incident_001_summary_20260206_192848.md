Incident Analysis Report: Frequency Spikes and Resulting Trips on Power Plant Blocks 1 and 2

1. Executive Summary

Between 10:05 and 10:07 UTC, frequency spikes were detected on PQM01 and PQM02 measurement points, which precipitated trips on blocks 1 and 2 of the power plant. This report analyzes the correlation between the PQM measurements, the Power Plant Controller (PPC) Master and Slave cores’ inputs and outputs, and associated error codes to identify the likely cause of the incident.

2. Incident Context and Data Overview

The incident involved monitoring of ten configured signals related to the PPC system and PQM measurements:

- PQM01_MEASUREMENT, PQM02_MEASUREMENT, PQM03_MEASUREMENT: Frequency and related parameters measured at three points of intersection within the plant.
- Inputs and outputs exchanged between the PPC Master core and three Slave cores (Slave 1, Slave 2, Slave 3).

Data artifacts include 10 data files and one document (which was unavailable for review). The time series data spans thousands of rows and process cycles, providing a comprehensive view of signal behavior before, during, and after the incident window.

3. Analysis of PQM Measurements

PQM01 and PQM02 measurements showed significant frequency spikes precisely within the 10:05–10:07 UTC window. These spikes are critical because:

- PQM01 and PQM02 are directly associated with blocks 1 and 2, respectively.
- The spikes exceeded normal operational thresholds, triggering protective trips on these blocks.

PQM03 measurements did not exhibit similar anomalies, indicating the issue was localized to the areas monitored by PQM01 and PQM02.

4. Correlation with PPC Master and Slave Core Inputs and Outputs

The PPC system architecture involves a Master core coordinating with multiple Slave cores to manage plant operations. Analysis of inputs and outputs reveals:

- Inputs sent to the Master core and Slave cores show consistent data flow with expected process cycles and line numbers, indicating no data loss or communication failure.
- Outputs received from Slave cores correspond closely with their inputs, suggesting the Slave cores processed commands without internal errors.
- No irregularities or error codes were reported in the data streams between Master and Slave cores during the incident timeframe.

This consistency implies that the PPC control logic and communication channels were operational and not the direct source of the frequency spikes.

5. Likely Cause of the Problem

Given the data, the most plausible cause of the frequency spikes and subsequent trips on blocks 1 and 2 is an external disturbance or fault localized at the points monitored by PQM01 and PQM02. Potential causes include:

- Sudden load changes or faults in the electrical network connected to blocks 1 and 2.
- Equipment malfunction or transient events affecting the measurement points.
- External grid disturbances impacting frequency stability at these points.

The PPC system’s Master and Slave cores functioned correctly, responding to the abnormal frequency inputs by initiating protective trips to safeguard the plant.

6. Recommendations

- Conduct a detailed inspection and maintenance check of equipment and connections at PQM01 and PQM02 locations to identify hardware faults.
- Review grid interaction logs to detect any external disturbances coinciding with the incident timeframe.
- Enhance monitoring and alerting thresholds for PQM measurements to enable earlier detection of frequency anomalies.
- Consider implementing redundancy or filtering mechanisms in measurement points to reduce false trips due to transient spikes.

7. Conclusion

The incident was triggered by frequency spikes detected at PQM01 and PQM02, which caused protective trips on blocks 1 and 2. The PPC Master and Slave cores operated as designed, processing inputs and outputs without error and executing trips in response to abnormal frequency signals. The root cause likely lies outside the PPC control system, related to localized disturbances or faults at the measurement points. Further investigation into equipment and grid conditions is warranted to prevent recurrence.
