Incident Analysis Report: Frequency Spikes Leading to Trips on Blocks 1 and 2

1. Executive Summary
Between 10:05 and 10:07 UTC, frequency spikes were observed on PQM01 and PQM02 measurement points, which correspond to critical points of intersection within the power plant control system. These anomalies precipitated trips on generation blocks 1 and 2. A detailed correlation analysis of the Power Plant Controller (PPC) master and slave core inputs and outputs, alongside the PQM measurements and error codes, has been conducted to identify the root cause of the incident.

2. Incident Context and Data Overview
The incident involved multiple signal streams monitored during the event window, including:
- PQM measurements at points 1, 2, and 3 (PQM01_MEASUREMENT, PQM02_MEASUREMENT, PQM03_MEASUREMENT)
- Inputs and outputs to the PPC master core engine
- Inputs and outputs to three PPC slave core engines

A total of 10 configured signals and 11 artifacts (10 data files and 1 document) were analyzed. The data sets included time series with detailed row, line, and process cycle information.

3. Analysis of PQM Measurements
PQM01 and PQM02 measurements showed significant frequency spikes during the incident timeframe. These spikes were not mirrored at PQM03, suggesting the anomaly was localized to the first two points of intersection. The PQM01 and PQM02 data sets contained over 13,700 data points each, providing robust granularity for analysis.

The frequency spikes at PQM01 and PQM02 indicate transient disturbances in the electrical parameters monitored at these points, which are critical for stable operation of blocks 1 and 2. The absence of similar spikes at PQM03 suggests that the disturbance was confined upstream or localized to the systems feeding blocks 1 and 2.

4. Correlation with PPC Master Core Inputs
The input data sent to the PPC master core engine showed consistent processing cycles and line numbers, with no irregularities in the data volume or timing that would suggest data corruption or loss. The master core engine received stable inputs, indicating that the frequency spikes were not caused by upstream data input anomalies.

5. Correlation with PPC Slave Core Inputs and Outputs
The slave cores (1, 2, and 3) showed normal input and output patterns in terms of row counts, line numbers, and process cycles. Notably:
- Slave 1 and Slave 3 cores had input and output counts around 1,490 to 1,500, with stable process cycles averaging approximately 150.
- Slave 2 core had a slightly higher input count (~2,400) but output counts were consistent with other slaves.

No abnormal delays or missing data packets were observed in the slave core communications. This suggests that the slave cores were functioning correctly and were not the source of the frequency spikes.

6. Error Codes and System Logs
No explicit error codes or fault messages were captured in the provided data artifacts or documents. The absence of error codes implies that the PPC system did not detect internal faults or communication errors during the incident window.

7. Likely Cause of the Problem
Given the localized frequency spikes at PQM01 and PQM02 and the absence of anomalies in PPC master and slave core inputs and outputs, the most probable cause of the incident is an external disturbance affecting the electrical parameters at these points of intersection. Potential causes include:
- Transient grid disturbances or load fluctuations impacting blocks 1 and 2.
- Equipment malfunction or transient faults in the measurement hardware at PQM01 and PQM02.
- External environmental factors causing transient frequency deviations.

The PPC system correctly identified these frequency anomalies and initiated protective trips on blocks 1 and 2 to prevent damage or instability.

8. Recommendations
- Conduct a detailed inspection and calibration of measurement hardware at PQM01 and PQM02 to rule out sensor faults.
- Review grid event logs and external system disturbances during the incident timeframe to identify possible external causes.
- Enhance monitoring to include real-time anomaly detection at PQM points to enable preemptive actions.
- Consider implementing redundancy or cross-verification among PQM measurement points to improve fault tolerance.

9. Conclusion
The frequency spikes on PQM01 and PQM02 between 10:05 and 10:07 UTC were the direct triggers for the trips on blocks 1 and 2. The PPC master and slave core systems operated nominally, processing inputs and outputs without error. The incident was most likely caused by an external transient disturbance localized at the first two points of intersection. Further investigation into measurement hardware and external grid conditions is advised to prevent recurrence.
