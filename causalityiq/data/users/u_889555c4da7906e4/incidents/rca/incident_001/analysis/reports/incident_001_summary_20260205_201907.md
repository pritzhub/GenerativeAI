**Incident Analysis Report: Frequency Spikes Leading to Trips on Blocks 1 and 2**

---

### 1. Executive Summary

Between 10:05 and 10:07 UTC, frequency spikes were detected on PQM01 and PQM02 measurement points, which directly resulted in trips on power generation blocks 1 and 2. This report analyzes the correlation between the PQM measurements, Power Plant Controller (PPC) Master and Slave core inputs and outputs, and error codes to identify the likely root cause of the incident.

---

### 2. Incident Context and Data Overview

- **Incident timeframe:** 10:05–10:07 UTC
- **Affected units:** Blocks 1 and 2
- **Key signals monitored:**  
  - PQM01_MEASUREMENT (Point of Intersection 1)  
  - PQM02_MEASUREMENT (Point of Intersection 2)  
  - PQM03_MEASUREMENT (Point of Intersection 3)  
  - Inputs and outputs to/from Master and Slave PPC cores (Slaves 1, 2, and 3)

- **Data volume:**  
  - PQM01: 13,719 data points  
  - PQM02: 13,744 data points  
  - PPC Master and Slave cores: Ranged from ~1,494 to 4,103 data points per signal

---

### 3. Detailed Signal Correlation and Observations

#### 3.1 PQM Measurements

- **PQM01 and PQM02** both recorded significant frequency spikes during the incident window. These spikes are abnormal deviations from nominal frequency values, indicating instability in the power output or grid conditions at these points.
- **PQM03** measurements did not show corresponding spikes, suggesting the issue was localized to the areas monitored by PQM01 and PQM02, which correspond to blocks 1 and 2.

#### 3.2 PPC Master Core Inputs

- The Master Core PPC engine received consistent inputs with a high data count (4,103 points), indicating normal operational data flow.
- No anomalies in input data volume or timing were observed outside the incident window, implying the Master PPC was receiving valid data.

#### 3.3 PPC Slave Cores Inputs and Outputs

- Slave cores 1, 2, and 3 showed normal input and output data volumes and timing, with no evident data loss or communication delays.
- Notably, Slave 1 and Slave 2 cores correspond to blocks 1 and 2, respectively. Their input and output data streams remained consistent, but the timing of their process cycles (~150 cycles mean) is significantly shorter than the Master core (~228 cycles mean), reflecting their role in localized control.

---

### 4. Error Codes and System Behavior

- No explicit error codes were captured or reported in the incident documentation.
- The absence of error codes suggests the PPC system did not detect internal faults but responded to external frequency anomalies by initiating protective trips on blocks 1 and 2.

---

### 5. Likely Cause Analysis

The frequency spikes on PQM01 and PQM02 are the primary triggers for the trips on blocks 1 and 2. The PPC system, designed to protect the plant from unstable operating conditions, reacted appropriately by tripping the affected blocks to prevent damage or unsafe operation.

**Potential root causes for the frequency spikes include:**

- **Grid disturbances or load fluctuations** localized to the areas monitored by PQM01 and PQM02.
- **Transient faults or switching events** in the power network affecting blocks 1 and 2.
- **Equipment malfunction or sensor anomalies** at PQM01 and PQM02 measurement points, though less likely given the simultaneous spikes and corresponding trips.

The PPC Master and Slave cores functioned correctly, processing inputs and outputs without communication or processing delays. The system’s protective logic triggered block trips based on the abnormal frequency measurements, indicating the PPC system operated as designed.

---

### 6. Recommendations

- **Grid Stability Review:** Coordinate with grid operators to investigate any disturbances or switching events during the incident window.
- **Sensor Verification:** Conduct calibration and health checks on PQM01 and PQM02 measurement devices to rule out sensor faults.
- **PPC System Monitoring:** Enhance real-time monitoring and logging of error codes and system states to capture more detailed diagnostics in future incidents.
- **Preventive Maintenance:** Review maintenance schedules for blocks 1 and 2 to ensure all protective and control systems are functioning optimally.

---

### 7. Conclusion

The incident involving frequency spikes on PQM01 and PQM02 led to protective trips on blocks 1 and 2. Analysis of PPC Master and Slave core inputs and outputs confirms that the PPC system responded correctly to abnormal frequency conditions. The most probable cause is an external grid disturbance or transient event affecting these blocks. Further investigation into grid events and sensor integrity is recommended to prevent recurrence.
